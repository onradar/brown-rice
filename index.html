What is Brown Rice?

Brown rice is a whole grain food that retains its bran and germ layer after milling. Unlike white rice, it has a chewy texture, a nutty flavor, and a significantly higher nutritional value.

Health Benefits of Brown Rice

Brown rice is rich in fiber, manganese, selenium, phosphorus, and B vitamins. Regular consumption supports healthy digestion, stabilizes blood sugar levels, and reduces the risk of type 2 diabetes.

How to Cook Brown Rice Perfectly

Use a 1:2.5 ratio (1 cup rice to 2.5 cups water). Bring to a boil, then simmer covered for 40–45 minutes. Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving.

Brown Rice vs White Rice

Brown rice contains 3x more fiber, more antioxidants, and a lower glycemic index than white rice — making it the superior choice for long-term health.

FAQ

Is brown rice gluten-free? Yes, brown rice is naturally gluten-free.

How long does cooked brown rice last? Up to 5 days in the refrigerator in an airtight container.

Can I eat brown rice every day? Yes — it is one of the healthiest staple grains you can eat daily.
