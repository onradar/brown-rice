Best Whole Grain Foods for a Healthy Diet

Published March 2026 · Whole Grains · 8 min read

Whole grains are one of the most consistently recommended food groups in nutrition science — yet most people eat far less than the recommended amount. This guide ranks the best whole grain foods by nutritional value, versatility, and practical use in everyday cooking.

What Makes a Food a Whole Grain?

A whole grain contains all three parts of the grain kernel: the bran (fiber, B vitamins), the germ (healthy fats, vitamins E and B), and the endosperm (carbohydrates, protein). Refined grains remove the bran and germ, leaving only the endosperm. Brown rice is a classic example of a whole grain — learn more about its specific health benefits here.

Brown Rice vs Other Whole Grains

Brown rice stands out for its versatility and availability. While quinoa edges it out on protein and barley on fiber, brown rice is easier to cook in large batches, works with almost any cuisine, and is significantly cheaper per serving. For detailed cooking instructions, see how to cook brown rice perfectly.

How Many Whole Grains Should You Eat?

The Dietary Guidelines for Americans recommend at least 3 servings of whole grains per day — where one serving equals roughly 1/2 cup of cooked grain. Most Americans currently eat less than one serving per day, which represents a major gap in nutritional intake.

Whole Grain vs Multigrain: Do Not Be Fooled

Products labeled multigrain contain multiple types of grain — but each grain may be refined. Always look for the word whole as the first ingredient: whole wheat, whole oats, whole grain brown rice. The label multigrain alone tells you nothing about fiber content.

Whole Grains and Weight Management

Replacing refined grains with whole grains is one of the most evidence-backed dietary strategies for weight management. A 2017 study in the American Journal of Clinical Nutrition found that adults who ate more whole grains had significantly lower body weight and waist circumference over time. If you are specifically using brown rice for weight loss, combining it with other whole grains amplifies the effect.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the healthiest whole grain foods?

Oats, quinoa, brown rice, barley, and whole wheat are among the most nutritious whole grains, each offering unique benefits.

How many servings of whole grains per day?

Health guidelines recommend at least 3 servings of whole grains per day for adults.

Are whole grains good for weight loss?

Yes. Whole grains are high in fiber and have a lower glycemic index than refined grains, supporting satiety and weight management.

What is the difference between whole grain and multigrain?

Whole grain means the entire grain kernel is used. Multigrain simply means multiple grains are present, but they may all be refined.

Is brown rice a whole grain?

Yes. Brown rice is a whole grain because it retains its bran, germ, and endosperm. White rice is not a whole grain.
